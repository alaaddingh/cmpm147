Artist Statement

Weaving Kufiyah turns the Palestinian scarf into an infinite, click-reactive playground. The sketch procedurally builds a black-net kufiyah across an endless field of diamond tiles. A single click drops a watermelon slice, a symbol of resilience and heritage, while some tiles are wrapped with olive buds.

The piece joins textile tradition with code. A 32-bit XXH32 hash of the world seed drives noiseSeed() and randomSeed(), guaranteeing that every key produces a repeatable, sharable pattern. Cross-hatched threads drift diagonally across the cloth, and a subtle tan discoloration is present via Perlin noise, giving the digital fabric a woven depth.

Where a real kufiyah has borders, this one is infinite and it keeps weaving.

Shukran to WesBot for wording assists :D

Technical Notes

The tile engine in engine.js draws a staggered isometric grid. For each tile (i,j) my_world.js hashes a few different strings to pick:

  • tan shade and cross-hatch offset
  • olive-sprig vs. dot decoration
  • animated watermelon phase when the tile is clicked

Because every decision is keyed to (i,j) + worldSeed, the landscape is both infinite and completely deterministic.

Reflection

This sketch grew out of a class demo on infinite scrolling worlds. Re-contextualising it as a cultural artefact made the math feel less abstract and a lot more personal. I gained inspiration from seeing other people's personal designs from Experiment2 and it inspired me to up the ante for my next project. I wanted all 3 of my generations to be about identity and I believe I successfully created 3 generators which embodies Levantine patterns and culture.
